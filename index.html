Projects

Kra-ken

About the game

Fight in intense, 1-on-1 battles with your friends! Kra-Ken is a 2.5D fighting game that will make you grip your controller ever more tightly as you beat your opponent.

Kra-Ken aims to emulate our own view of the Street Fighter legacy, and pay homage to the many years of inspiration and joy it has brought by putting a twist on the existing formula. Experience the two unique characters and stages as envisioned by DGTL Dojo.


My contribution

This project was made during my third year at BUAS and had a 32 Week development time. Within this project I was responsible for gameplay design (applying hurt- and hitboxes to our characters' attacks and moves) and gameplay balance (the balancing of our character's damage, movement speed and other data).

Canine 49

About the game

In Canine 49, you play as Luther - a 20-year old mechanic - who lives happily together with his dog Scrappy. That is until one day Scrappy breaks down. Now it is up to Luther to find the parts to fix poor ol' Scrappy and continue their happy life together.


About the client

This prompt was sent through the Rainbow Connection - a wish granting organization located in Michigan. The original author of the concept is Nathan Stewart, an 18-year-old who was brought in to contact with BUAS through the Rainbow Connection.


My contribution

This game was made during my second year at BUAS. In the three months of development for this game I worked on the open world puzzles within the game. I would concept several scenarios on paper, which I then tested with playtester, and then made them functional within our game.
